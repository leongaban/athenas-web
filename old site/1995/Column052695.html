  Week of May 26th - June 1st, 1995

The Seventh Seal

(The Eleventh in a Series on The Seven Seals)

Revelation

   "The Lamb then broke the seventh seal, and there was silence in heaven for about half an hour."          -Revelation 8:1

The last of the Seals
Delta Piscium: 1054 AD
   As we continue to calibrate the passage of the Vernal Equinox over the seven stars (seals) of Revelation, we find delta Piscium aligned with this celestial marker in 1054 AD. The seven stars that we have been studying represent a portion of Pisces, specifically the first seven stars along the stream of water leading to the more southern of the two fish of this constellation. This collection of seven stars represents a group which was designated in China as Wae Ping, the Rolled Screen (referred to as 'a scroll rolling up' in Revelation 6:14).

   After delta Piscium there is a gap in heaven until we reach the next star in the constellation. If we were to watch each of the stars in the 'scroll' cross the horizon and mark their timings, thirteen minutes would elapse between delta and epsilon, ten minutes between epsilon and zeta, etc.

Wae Ping, the Rolled Screen
including alpha, xi, mu, nu, zeta, epsilon and delta Piscium
The remaining stars would take thirteen, thirteen, nineteen, and nine minutes respectively to rise. But between delta (the seventh seal) and omega, the next star in our series, there is a gap of 48 minutes. The opening of the seventh seal calls for a 'silence in heaven for about half an hour'. Understand that those in antiquity did not use the standardized time notations that we do today, and a half hour varied from 20 to 37 minutes in length throughout the year. Thus the designation, 'about a half hour' (48 minutes compared to 37) indicates the lack of celestial activity (stars) along the constellation as marked by the Vernal Equinox. The name astrology etymologically translates as logos, the 'word' of the astrum, 'star' or stars. Astrology is the language of the stars. The lack of stellar activity along the Vernal Equinox simply indicates that for about a half an hour, heaven was not speaking to us, or there was a 'silence in heaven'.

Jupiter
chief god of the Romans
   Thus far in our examination, the opening of the seven seals have generally indicated a period of disintegration and social chaos in the development of Roman history. Rome has represented the terrestrial axis around which our civilization has turned for over the last two thousand years. It is the social mortar that has bonded us together, first through the establishment of the Republic and Empire, and then through the power of Christianity and the Church. Was there anything of significance that occurred in 1054 which seems to fit this theme?

Pope Leo IX
   The Schism of 1054 is marked as the year of the final cleavage between the Eastern and Western Churches which had continued to unite the old Roman Empire. Although politically and militarily divided for many centuries, the Greek notion of oikowmene, of one universal Christian society, had persevered, although weathered, until this time. But differences in philosophy, language, and culture contributed toward the tendency to drift apart, and when Rome insisted on Constantinople's compliance and obedience, the rift became final as each side excommunicated the other for what they perceived as outrageous acts.

   As the opening of the first seal marked the start of Rome's ascendancy, so the seventh seal marked the final cleavage of the church.
