Athena's Web Weekly Column

  Week of October 29th - November 4th,  1990

War Watch

Gulf War Archive

   "War in the Persian Gulf. November 7, 1990. US vs. Iraq."

US Flag
   We are approaching the second date that was mentioned at the beginning of September in the Web, and here we are recalling your attention to it. We said that there were going to be two significant flash points in Iraq's chart, one of which was the 22nd of September, and the other the 7th of November, 1990. We are now on the eve of that latter date, and the indicators are coming in from various quarters that our administration is seeing the current situation as bogged down and in need of dramatic action.

Saddam Hussein
   It so happens that these dates are right after the elections, and that political considerations have been one of the reasons we haven't moved already. Even though we first thought that Iraq might be the perpetrator, closer scrutiny and increasing information would indicate that they are going to be the recipients rather than the initiators. What our administration does not realize is that this action, rather than freeing the economy, will send it into a recession from which we will not return for many years.

   Webheads who are regular readers will recollect that we "hit" on the first date, as tensions escalated right around the 22nd of September, with Hussein threatening to bring Israel into the conflict, blow up the oil fields, and strike first if the embargo was carried too far, all from the 21st to the 24th. Since that time, events seem to have settled back down again. This was part of a package prediction, the first of two dates. This is the other date.

   The Web certainly hasn't been one hundred percent accurate on its predictions in the past, although we have had an inordinate number of correct hits. Personally, it is my hope that we're wrong on this one, and it could be that this will once again simply indicate an escalation of tensions without our coming to blows.

But I don't think so. Those of you who are so inclined, please pray for the best possible outcome for this situation. Try not to specify a specific outcome, but just pray for the best.

   What else can we do?
