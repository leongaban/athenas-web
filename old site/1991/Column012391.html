  Week of January 23rd - January 29th,  1991

Play It Again, George

Gulf War Archive

   It seems that we are always learning the lessons of the past;

US Flag
getting ready once and for all to fight the last war.

Pres George Bush
In the WWII there was the Magnot Line, with state of the art technology and concrete encasements; it was the answer to the trench warfare problems of WWI. The only trouble was that the Germans didn't have sense enough to know that, and they simply went around it.

   This time, and in this war, we're going through a bit of a time warp. We are not only refighting the last war, we're replaying two of the last three wars. First, we're assured that this is not going to be another Vietnam. We will not tie the hands of our troops, and they will have the full support of everything that we can give them. The terrain is different, the enemy is different, the technology is different. Hussein doesn't have a chance. Or so we are told.

   But at the same time, we're also getting a large slice of WWII being thrown into the mix. This time were going to take care of Hitler immediately after he moves into Czechoslovakia. We have seen what appeasement can do, and to the disastrous consequence that it can lead to. George Bush will be no Neville Chamberlain.

Neville Chamberlain
You can count on that.

   As February folds into March, the real price of war will become apparent.

Learning from the lessons of history as taught to us by the White House, there will be no compromise, no umbrella and no worthless pieces of paper flying in the wind. We should do everything in our power to avoid anything that Chamberlain attempted. At least now we can be rest assured that there will be no "peace in our time."
