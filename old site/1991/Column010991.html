Athena's Web Weekly Column

  Week of January 9th - January 15th,  1991

Deadline

Gulf War Archive

   As we approach the January 15th deadline set by the UN and the U.S. for Iraq to get out of Kuwait, everyone is waiting with baited breath to see just what will happen. The Iraqis want to talk about the larger range of issues that are now being faced in the Middle East; addressing some real concerns, but at the same time trying to take some of the heat off themselves. What can we expect on the 15th of January? Will there be war, or will there be peace?

US Flag
   My personal opinion is that there will be a heated verbal exchange, with a significant rising in tensions on the 15th, but that a major offensive will not in fact be tied to this date. As we have picked out the flash points before, the next set of flash points will be at the end of February and the beginning of March. February 26 and March 6, as well as the day of the Full Moon, will hold the key to the cards of the future. What transpires on each of those days will determine where the Persian Gulf crisis is headed.

The February Full Moon
   Based on the historical evidence, this is a time that the U.S. president pushes us in a direction that is fiscally unsound for the country. Just how far we are pushed remains to be seen. It is possible that what we are looking at here is simply the accumulation of the S&L crisis, bank failures and other financial woes, and let's hope that's all were seeing. But given additional celestial indicators from the U.S. map, it's my opinion that if we do in fact initiate military action at this time, the results will be far more costly than we presently realize. The U.S. progressed Moon will be Void of Course at the end of February, and the initiation of substantial activities will be dubious at best.
   These celestial indicators do not predispose us to any given course of action. Our free will is still our own. Let us pray at this time that George Bush knows what he is doing, because once we cross the deadline, there may be no turning back.
